# Go-To-Market Strategy — StackAudit (Credex)

Target Persona
- Primary: Engineering Managers at Series A startups (20-150 employees). These managers control tool procurement approvals for engineering teams and are highly sensitive to recurring developer tooling spend. They typically balance delivering product velocity with headcount efficiency and have concrete budgeting cycles tied to runway.
- Secondary: CTOs at later-stage startups undergoing rapid tooling consolidation and procurement teams responsible for vendor negotiation.

Problem & Friction Points
- Fragmented billing: Organizations accrue multiple AI tool subscriptions (IDE plugins, LLM APIs, research tools) spread across corporate cards, individual invoices, and team-level subscriptions, causing poor visibility.
- Seat misalignment: Teams often provision enterprise or team-tier licenses without aligning seats to active users, creating per-seat waste.
- API spend leakage: High-volume API calls billed at retail rates without wholesale contractual protections, leading to significant monthly overages.

Value Proposition
- StackAudit finds immediate, defensible cost savings using a deterministic audit engine and yields rapid wins (seat consolidation, tier downgrades, routing to wholesale credits). For engineering leaders, this translates to runway preservation and reduced procurement friction.

Zero-dollar Outbound Distribution Loop (First 100 users)
1. Community-Led Content: Publish a short, actionable audit template blog post and a 6-minute demo video on Product Hunt and Hacker News showcasing a real-world seat consolidation that saved $2k/mo. Include a public link to a live demo of StackAudit pre-filled with anonymized sample data.

2. Developer First Outreach (Organic): Identify 200 targeted Series A startups via Crunchbase filtered by funding date. Use public engineering manager names on LinkedIn. Send a personalized message referencing a specific pain (“noticed your stack includes Copilot + multiple API vendors — ran a quick audit, see potential savings”). Offer a free 15-minute audit walkthrough. No ad spend.

3. Partnership Loop: Partner with 2 developer-focused newsletters (e.g., JavaScript Weekly, LLM Weekly) to offer an exclusive audit template and invite readers to try the live audit. Provide a sharable “audit snapshot” URL that invites recipients to try the product — viral by design.

4. Referral Incentive (Non-monetary): Offer early access to Credex wholesale waitlist for referrals. For every 2 successful referrals who request a consultation, unlock a 30-minute procurement playbook session.

5. Product-Led Retention: When a user hits an identified savings threshold (e.g., > $500/mo), present a high-converting CTA to schedule a Credex wholesale consultation. Use that enterprise conversation to convert to paid consulting or wholesale contracts.

Metrics & KPIs
- Activation: Demo audit completed (target 40% conversion from signup to completed audit).
- Qualification: Percentage of audits with > $500/mo identified savings (target 12–18% in first cohort).
- Conversion: Consultation requests to paid engagement (target 10% over first 6 months).

Execution Timeline (First 90 days)
- Week 1–2: Launch demo, publish blog, seed Product Hunt listing.
- Week 3–6: One-on-one outreach to 200 Series A targets; schedule demo audits.
- Week 7–12: Run pilot consultations, document case studies, and refine messaging.

Closing
- This zero-dollar loop focuses on high-intent developer channels and product-led conversion. The combination of defensible math, public baseline pricing (transparent citations), and a direct consultation CTA creates a low-friction path to enterprise conversations without upfront ad spend.
